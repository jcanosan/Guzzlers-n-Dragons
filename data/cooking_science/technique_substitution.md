# Technique Substitution Guide

## Thickening Agents
When a recipe calls for one thickener but you need to substitute:

**Cornstarch → Arrowroot**
- Use 1:1 ratio
- Arrowroot creates clearer sauce, works at lower temps
- Don't use with dairy (gets slimy)

**Cornstarch → Tapioca Starch**
- Use 1:1 ratio
- Better freeze-thaw stability
- Slightly chewy texture

**Flour → Cornstarch**
- Use half the amount (1 tbsp flour = 1/2 tbsp cornstarch)
- Cornstarch has 2x thickening power
- Mix with cold liquid first to avoid lumps

**Flour → Arrowroot**
- Use half the amount
- Clearer result, more neutral flavor

**Gelatin → Agar**
- Use 1:1 by weight (agar sets firmer)
- Agar sets at room temp, gelatin needs refrigeration
- Agar doesn't melt in mouth same way

**Gelatin → Pectin**
- For fruit-based gels only
- Pectin needs acid and sugar to set
- Different mouthfeel

**Xanthan Gum → Guar Gum**
- Use 1:1 ratio
- Guar better for cold applications
- Xanthan better for acidic/hot

**Reduction → Any Thickener**
- Simmer to evaporate water, concentrates flavor
- No added ingredients, but changes flavor profile
- Takes time, can over-reduce

## Leavening Substitutions

**Baking Powder → Baking Soda + Acid**
- 1 tsp baking powder = 1/4 tsp baking soda + 1/2 tsp cream of tartar
- Or 1/4 tsp baking soda + 1/2 cup buttermilk/yogurt (reduce other liquid)
- Reaction starts immediately, bake fast

**Yeast → Baking Powder (Quick Breads Only)**
- 1 packet yeast (2 1/4 tsp) = 2-3 tsp baking powder
- Texture will be more cake-like, less chewy
- No rise time needed

**Egg Whites (foam) → Aquafaba**
- 1 egg white = 3 tbsp aquafaba (chickpea liquid)
- Whips similarly, slightly less stable
- Good for meringues, mousses

## Cooking Method Substitutions

**Deep Fry → Air Fry / Oven**
- Coat with oil spray, cook at 400°F/200°C
- Less oil absorption, different texture
- May need longer time

**Slow Cooker → Dutch Oven (Oven)**
- Low setting (200°F/90°C) = 275°F/135°C oven
- High setting (300°F/150°C) = 300°F/150°C oven
- Check liquid levels (less evaporation in slow cooker)

**Grill → Broiler / Cast Iron**
- Broiler: 4-6 inches from element, watch closely
- Cast iron: very hot, press food for grill marks
- Both give char but no smoke flavor

**Steam → Microwave with Water**
- Cover dish with damp paper towel or lid + splash water
- Fast but less even
- Good for vegetables, fish

## Fat Substitutions in Baking

**Butter → Oil**
- Use 3/4 cup oil per 1 cup butter
- Oil = moister, denser crumb
- No water content in oil

**Butter → Applesauce/Mashed Banana**
- Use 1:1 ratio for up to half the butter
- Reduces fat, adds moisture and sweetness
- Changes texture to more cake-like

**Butter → Greek Yogurt**
- Use 1/2 cup yogurt per 1 cup butter
- Adds tang, moisture, protein
- Reduce other liquids slightly

## Dairy Substitutions

**Heavy Cream → Milk + Butter**
- 1 cup cream = 3/4 cup milk + 1/4 cup melted butter
- Won't whip, but works in cooking

**Buttermilk → Milk + Acid**
- 1 cup buttermilk = 1 cup milk + 1 tbsp lemon juice/vinegar
- Let sit 5 minutes

**Sour Cream → Greek Yogurt**
- 1:1 ratio
- Yogurt tangier, less fat
- Good in baking, dips, toppings
