# Flavor Pairing Science

## The Flavor Pairing Hypothesis
Ingredients sharing key volatile compounds tend to pair well. This explains classic and surprising combinations.

## Classic Pairings by Compound Class

### Esters (Fruity)
- **Ethyl butanoate**: Pineapple + apricot, strawberry + pineapple
- **Ethyl hexanoate**: Apple + anise, pear + blue cheese
- **Isoamyl acetate**: Banana + chocolate, banana + rum

### Terpenes (Herbal/Citrus)
- **Limonene**: Orange + dark chocolate, lemon + thyme, grapefruit + rosemary
- **Pinene**: Rosemary + lamb, pine nuts + basil, juniper + gin
- **Linalool**: Lavender + honey, coriander + citrus, basil + tomato

### Pyrazines (Nutty/Roasted)
- **2,5-Dimethylpyrazine**: Chocolate + coffee, peanut + chocolate
- **2,3-Diethyl-5-methylpyrazine**: Beef + mushrooms, roasted vegetables

### Sulfur Compounds (Allium/Brassica)
- **Allicin**: Garlic + tomato, onion + beef
- **Methanethiol**: Asparagus + egg, cabbage + caraway
- **Dimethyl sulfide**: Corn + butter, truffle + potato, seafood + corn

## Flavor Bridge Technique
To combine two ingredients that don't share compounds, use a "bridge" ingredient that shares compounds with both.

Examples:
- **Chocolate + Blue Cheese** → Bridge: Walnut (shares compounds with both)
- **Strawberry + Basil** → Bridge: Tomato or balsamic vinegar
- **Coffee + Orange** → Bridge: Cardamom or dark chocolate
- **Watermelon + Feta** → Bridge: Mint or cucumber
- **Pear + Gorgonzola** → Bridge: Walnut or honey

## Flavor Families

### Sweet
- Vanilla, caramel, honey, maple, molasses, brown sugar, coconut, sweet spices (cinnamon, nutmeg, cardamom)

### Salty/Umami
- Soy sauce, miso, fish sauce, parmesan, anchovies, mushrooms, tomato paste, seaweed, cured meats

### Sour/Acidic
- Lemon, lime, vinegar (all types), yogurt, buttermilk, tamarind, sumac, rhubarb, green apple

### Bitter
- Coffee, dark chocolate, cocoa nibs, grapefruit, kale, radicchio, endive, beer, quinine, gentian

### Spicy/Pungent
- Black pepper, chili, ginger, horseradish, wasabi, mustard, garlic, onion, cumin, coriander seed

### Herbal/Floral
- Basil, mint, thyme, rosemary, sage, oregano, tarragon, lavender, rose, elderflower, chamomile

## Pairing Principles

### 1. Shared Compounds = Natural Harmony
- Tomato + basil (shared linalool, methyl eugenol)
- Chocolate + coffee (shared pyrazines)
- Strawberry + cream (shared esters + fat carries flavor)

### 2. Contrast Creates Interest
- Sweet + salty (salt enhances sweet perception)
- Fat + acid (acid cuts richness)
- Spicy + cooling (mint, yogurt, cucumber)
- Bitter + sweet (chocolate + sugar, coffee + cream)

### 3. Regional Pairings = Cultural Wisdom
- Mediterranean: tomato + olive oil + garlic + basil
- Mexican: chili + lime + corn + cilantro
- Indian: cumin + coriander + turmeric + ginger
- Thai: fish sauce + lime + chili + palm sugar + herbs
- Japanese: soy + mirin + dashi + ginger

### 4. Texture Pairings
- Crunchy + creamy (nuts in ice cream, croutons in soup)
- Chewy + crisp (dried fruit in granola)
- Smooth + textured (puree + garnish)

## Substitution Guidelines

### When replacing an ingredient:
1. **Match the dominant flavor family** (sweet for sweet, umami for umami)
2. **Consider the chemical role** (acid for acid, fat for fat, thickener for thickener)
3. **Check for bridge ingredients** that connect to other recipe components
4. **Test in small batches** - perception is subjective

### Common Substitution Pairs:
- **Fish sauce** → Soy sauce + anchovy paste (umami + salt)
- **Mirin** → Sake + sugar (sweet rice wine)
- **Tamarind** → Lime + brown sugar + date paste (sour + sweet + fruit)
- **Gochujang** → Miso + chili flakes + honey + sesame oil
- **Harissa** → Chili paste + caraway + coriander + garlic
- **Za'atar** → Thyme + sesame + sumac + oregano
- **Herbes de Provence** → Thyme + rosemary + marjoram + savory + lavender
